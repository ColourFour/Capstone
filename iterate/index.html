What is conditional probability?

Conditional probability is the probability of an event happening given that another event has already happened.

P(A | B) = P(A ∩ B) / P(B) (when P(B) > 0)

How to think about it

  • “Given B” means your sample space shrinks to only outcomes where B happened.
  • Then you ask: within that smaller world, how often does A happen?

Quick example (numbers)

Suppose P(A)=0.3, P(B)=0.5, and P(A ∩ B)=0.15.

Then P(A | B)=0.15/0.5=0.3.

Same probability as P(A) here — but that’s not always the case.

Quick example (sample space)

Roll two fair dice.

Given that the sum is 8, the possible outcomes are: (2,6) (3,5) (4,4) (5,3) (6,2).

So P(first die is 3 | sum is 8) = 1/5.
